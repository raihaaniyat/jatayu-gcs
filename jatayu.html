Jatayu: Integrated Air-Ground SAR & Mission Control
MITS-DU Gwalior Madhav Institute of Technology and Science
Project Jatayu

An integrated autonomous SAR platform combining a hybrid VTOL drone for aerial surveillance, a real-time GCS for mission coordination, and a ground rover with a robotic arm + LiDAR for close-proximity ground operations.

Why It Matters

In disaster scenarios, the first 72 hours are critical. The VTOL rapidly locates survivors from above, the GCS coordinates in real time, and the rover physically intervenes on the ground, all working as a unified system.

VTOL DRONE: AERIAL SURVEILLANCE
VTOL Drone

A hybrid VTOL that launches vertically, transitions to fixed-wing for range, and loiters over areas of interest. Streams live video processed by YOLOv8 for real-time human detection. Survivors are auto-geotagged via GPS, with posture classification (upright vs prone) to prioritize distressed individuals.

VTOL Drone: Hybrid Build
VTOL Capabilities
  • Vertical take-off & landing, no runway needed
  • Fixed-wing cruise for extended range and endurance
  • Live MJPEG stream with YOLO bounding boxes
  • Posture classification for survivor prioritization
  • GPS geotagging of all detected targets
  • Payload delivery via servo release

GROUND CONTROL STATION: MISSION COMMAND
GCS: Real-Time Mission Control

A full-featured ground station built with React + Vite and FastAPI. Displays live telemetry, streams AI-processed video, manages geotagged targets, coordinates payload drops, and provides a tactical map with real-time drone tracking.

System Architecture
GCS Mission Control
GCS Features
  • Live telemetry (GPS, altitude, heading, battery, speed)
  • AI video stream with real-time YOLO bounding boxes
  • Target management with auto-geotag and severity
  • Tactical map with drone, GCS, targets, and distances
  • Payload drop via servo release at target coordinates
  • Flight commands (GUIDED, AUTO, RTL, LOITER, QLOITER)

SAR GROUND ROVER: ROBOTIC ARM + LiDAR SLAM
Ground Rover: SAR Ground Ops

Operates at the point of need, navigating rubble, collapsed structures, and narrow passages where drones cannot reach. Carries a robotic arm manipulator for physical interaction and a LiDAR sensor for real-time mapping in GPS-denied environments.

Rover in Action
Robotic Arm Manipulator

A multi-DOF robotic arm for ground-level SAR tasks: clearing debris, delivering medical supplies, actuating mechanisms, and retrieving hazardous objects. Powered by ROS 2 + MoveIt 2 with perception-driven grasp planning.

Robotic Arm in Action
LiDAR SLAM: GPS-Denied Navigation

GPS is often unavailable indoors and in dense rubble. The rover's LiDAR performs SLAM via ROS 2 SLAM Toolbox, building real-time occupancy grid maps from point cloud data while tracking position within the map.

Rover SLAM Pipeline
Rover SAR Capabilities
  • LiDAR SLAM for GPS-denied mapping
  • Robotic arm for debris clearance and supply delivery
  • Autonomous navigation through rubble and tight spaces
  • ROS 2 with MoveIt 2, Nav2, SLAM Toolbox
  • Reaches where drones cannot (indoors, underground)
